Shrimp and Asparagus Stir-Fry

Get a lot of flavor with just five ingredients. BY TINA MARTINEZPUBLISHED:APR 21, 2024 Star BE THE FIRST TO REVIEW! JUMP TO RECIPE bookmarksSAVE RECIPE

Gochujang creates a quick flavor-packed sauce for juicy shrimp and just-tender asparagus. The result is a 5-ingredient meal that pops with savory-sweet heat.





Yields: 4 Total Time: 25 mins Cal/Serv: 142





Ingredients

    2 Tbsp. gochujang 1 Tbsp. olive oil 1 lb. asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces 3 scallions, white and light green parts cut into 2-inch pieces, dark green parts thinly sliced Kosher salt 1 lb. large peeled and deveined shrimp 1 Tbsp. fresh lemon juice plus lemon wedges, for serving SHOP RECIPE Powered by Chicory



    Directions

      Step 1 In small bowl, stir together gochujang and 3 to 4 tablespoons water; set aside. Step 2 Heat 1/2 tablespoon oil in large skillet on medium-high. Add asparagus, scallions white and light green parts, and 1/4 teaspoon salt and cook, without stirring, 2 minutes. Toss and continue cooking, tossing occasionally, until just tender, 2 to 3 minutes more; transfer to plate and wipe out skillet. Step 3 Heat remaining 1/2 tablespoon oil on medium. Add shrimp and cook, without stirring, 2 minutes. Flip and cook 1 minute more. Return asparagus and scallions to skillet along with gochujang sauce and cook, tossing, until shrimp is opaque throughout and coated in sauce, 30 to 60 seconds. Step 4 Remove from heat and toss with lemon juice. Sprinkle with dark scallion greens and serve with lemon wedges if desired.




    Creamy Kale Pasta

    Cottage cheese makes this pasta extra creamy. BY JOY CHO AND SUSAN CHOUNGPUBLISHED: MAR 10, 2023 Star FillStar FillStar FillStar FillHalf Star 4.5 2 RATINGS JUMP TO RECIPE bookmarksSAVE RECIPE

    For a super easy, nut-free take on pesto, we swapped in cottage cheese. It adds creaminess and body (and a good dose of protein!). Be sure to reserve some pasta cooking water for tossing the saucy pasta with kale before serving.





    Yields: 4 Total Time: 25 mins Cal/Serv: 445

    Ingredients
      12 oz. short pasta (like orecchiette or gemelli) 2 scallions, roughly chopped 1 5-oz pkg baby kale 1/. cottage cheese 1/3 c. grated Parmesan, plus more for serving Kosher salt and pepper 2 Tbsp. extra virgin olive oil SHOP RECIPE Powered by Chicory




    Directions
      Step 1 Cook pasta per package. directions. Reserve 1/2 cup cooking water, drain, and return pasta to pot. Step 2 While pasta cooks, in food processor, pulse scallions and 3 cups kale to finely chop. Add cottage cheese, Parmesan and 1/2 teaspoon each salt and pepper; pulse to combine. Step 3 Scrape down sides, then with machine running, gradually add oil and puree until smooth. Step 4 Toss pasta with sauce to coat, then toss with remaining 3 cups kale, adding a couple tablespoons reserved pasta water as necessary to help kale wilt. Serve topped with additional Parmesan and freshly cracked pepper.




    Samosa Salad

    A delicious meatless main! BY TINA MARTINEZPUBLISHED: APR 19, 2024 Star BE THE FIRST TO REVIEW! JUMP TO RECIPE bookmarks SAVE RECIPE

    Skip the croutons and cut golden samosas into bite-size pieces for a delightfully fun and flavorful salad topper. Mango chutney whisked into the dressing provides a tangy-sweet boost.

    Yields: 4 Total Time: 30 mins Cal/Serv: 442





    Ingredients

      14 oz. potato and pea samosas (12 to 16 small) 2 medium yellow onions, sliced into 1/4-inch-thick wedges 4 Tbsp. olive oil, divided Kosher salt 1/2 jalapeño, finely chopped 3 Tbsp. mango chutney 2 Tbsp. white wine vinegar 1 c. frozen peas, thawed 1 5-ounce container baby spinach 1/2 c. cilantro leaves, roughly chopped 1/3 c. mint leaves, roughly chopped




    Directions

      Step 1 Heat oven to 375°F. On rimmed baking sheet, bake samosas until golden brown and warmed through, 20 to 25 minutes (depending on brand). Transfer to cutting board and cut each into 3 pieces. Step 2 Meanwhile, on separate rimmed baking sheet, toss onions with 2 tablespoons oil and 1/4 teaspoon each salt and pepper. Bake along with samosas 15 minutes. Toss onions with jalapeño and bake until onion is browned in spots, about 5 minutes more. Step 3 Meanwhile, in small bowl, whisk together mango chutney, vinegar, and remaining 2 tablespoons oil. Step 4 Toss onions with peas and spinach and bake until peas are warm and spinach begins to wilt, 1 to 2 minutes. Toss with cilantro and mint and 1/4 teaspoon salt. Divide spinach salad among plates, top with samosas and drizzle with dressing.
